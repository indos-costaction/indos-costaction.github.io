## WG3 Training School

The Working Group 3 (Automated Preprocessing Pipelines) Training School is the Group's first
in-person working meeting — two or three days of hands-on, collaborative work in Madrid.

- **When:** A meeting of **2 or 3 days** in **autumn 2026** — the date and length are being
  finalised. **[Pre-register](#pre-registration-intent-to-attend)** to help us choose the date.
- **Where:** Room COLABORA, Espacio Converge, Campus Serrano del CSIC, c/ Serrano 113 posterior, Madrid, Spain
- **Who:** Open pre-registration. Around 20–25 places.
- **Cost:** Nothing to attend, and **travel and subsistence are covered** for eligible
  participants — see [Reimbursement](#reimbursement).

**[INDoS](https://www.cost.eu/actions/CA24161/)** (Improving Neuroimaging Data for Sharing,
COST Action CA24161) is a network of researchers across more than 30 countries working to make
shared neuroimaging data genuinely reusable — by improving data quality assessment,
preprocessing, metadata, and the ethics and legal practice of sharing.
**[Working Group 3](/wg3)** is the part of that network that handles **automated preprocessing
pipelines**: it maps out established preprocessing procedures — initially for EEG and MEG,
drawing on established MRI practice — toward transparent, reproducible, and interoperable
pipelines.

This meeting is where that work actually gets done. It advances the Group's three task forces
and seeds the deliverables of the first Grant Period.

## Programme

The meeting is a **working** meeting, not a lecture series: three task-force blocks in which
participants build the Group's outputs together, plus an optional hands-on session.

- **Tools and workflows inventory (TF1).** Build the taxonomy behind WG3's inventory of
  preprocessing tools and workflows across modalities — what exists, how it is classified, and
  what the expert annotations should capture.
- **Literature review (TF2).** Agree the scope, the pre-registration, and the coding protocol
  for a systematic review of how preprocessing and its provenance are reported.
- **Training materials (TF3).** Plan the Action's preprocessing training resources — what the
  community needs and who builds it.
- **Co-working / hackathon.** An optional hands-on session prototyping against the tool
  inventory.

The detailed schedule below is the working template; **calendar dates follow the date decision**.
In a **2-day** event the co-working session is an optional Day-2 afternoon; in a **3-day** event
it becomes a full Day 3 and the concluding wrap-up moves to the end.

- From 09:15 to 11:00 - Task Force 3 (Training): training activities — synthesis and next steps
- From 11:00 to 11:15 - Coffee
- From 11:15 to 13:00 - Task Force 3 (Training): training materials roadmap and next steps
- From 13:00 to 14:00 - Lunch
- From 14:00 to 15:45 - Task Force 2 (Literature review): scope, review pre-registration, modality subgroups
- From 15:45 to 16:00 - Coffee
- From 16:00 to 18:00 - Task Force 2 (Literature review): review protocol and reviewer assignment

### Day 2

- From 09:00 to 10:30 - Task Force 1 (Tools and workflows inventory): scope and taxonomy
- From 10:30 to 10:45 - Coffee
- From 10:45 to 12:15 - Task Force 1 (Tools and workflows inventory): survey design and inventory outline

- From 13:00 to 14:00 - Lunch
- From 14:00 to 18:00 - Open co-working and hacking session — optional (2-day) or the start of Day 3 (3-day)

### Day 3 *(3-day format only)*

- A full hands-on co-working / hackathon day on the tools-and-workflows inventory, closing with
  the concluding wrap-up.

## Pre-registration (intent to attend)

**[→ Pre-register here](https://limesurvey.hes-so.ch/index.php/236675?lang=en)** — by
**25 July 2026, 01:00 CEST**.

The date and length of the meeting are **not yet fixed**, and the pre-registration form is how we
choose them. There are around **20–25 places** (we may be able to welcome more if resources
permit).

- You score several **candidate dates** as *Yes / If need be / No*, and say whether you'd prefer
  a **2-day or 3-day** event.
- Once the organisers pick the final date, **everyone who marked it *Yes* or *If need be* is moved
  from pre-registered to registered** (subject to capacity, in priority order).
- If you didn't pick the chosen date, you can **still register afterwards** — you simply join the
  back of the queue.

Because marking a slot *Yes* or *If need be* is what decides the date, please only mark slots you
could genuinely make.

### Who can take part

**Anyone may pre-register.** To have your costs reimbursed you need to be a registered INDoS
member with an affiliation in a COST Member, Near-Neighbour, or International Partner Country —
see [Reimbursement](#reimbursement). Not yet a member? [Joining INDoS](/join) is free and takes a
few minutes; do it before the event to be reimbursable. If your affiliation falls outside the
COST countries, you are welcome to join us self-funded, seats permitting. If we are
oversubscribed, registered WG3 members have priority.

> **Pre-registration is not a reimbursement guarantee.** It records your intent to attend and is
> separate from e-COST. Reimbursement requires INDoS membership and an **e-COST invitation** that
> you accept — the local organiser issues these **after** the date is fixed.

Need to change or withdraw your pre-registration? Email
[oscar.esteban@hes-so.ch](mailto:oscar.esteban@hes-so.ch) before the deadline.

## Reimbursement

**Travel and subsistence are covered** for eligible participants, through the standard COST
mechanism. Reimbursement comprises a **flat-rate daily allowance** (per the COST daily-allowance
table for the host country, covering accommodation, meals, and local transport — no receipts
needed) **plus travel costs up to a total of €1,500**. There is no registration fee.

To be reimbursed you must be a registered INDoS member affiliated with a COST Member,
Near-Neighbour, or International Partner Country; accept the **e-COST invitation** the local
organiser issues once the date is fixed; sign the attendance list at the event; and submit the
Online Travel Reimbursement Request (OTRR) in e-COST within 30 days of the event. See the
[COST Annotated Rules](https://www.cost.eu/cost-actions/annotated-rules-qa/) and the
[e-COST platform](https://e-services.cost.eu/) for details.
